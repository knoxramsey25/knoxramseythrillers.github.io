What Makes This Different

Technical Authenticity

Real industrial control systems. Real vulnerabilities. Knox Ramsey navigates the actual SCADA networks, PLCs, and HMI interfaces that control America's food production—from nitrogen injection ratios in vertical farms to pH sensors in hydroponic bays.

No Hollywood hacking. Just the terrifying reality of how exposed our critical infrastructure really is.

The Supply Chain as Weapon

The attack vector isn't a bomb or a virus—it's the "recipe" itself. Subtle manipulations in agricultural setpoints, cold chain logistics, and processing parameters turn the entire food system into a delivery mechanism.

When every control point becomes a potential weapon, how do you defend a continent-wide network?

An Engineer's Mindset

Knox Ramsey isn't a super-spy or ex-military. He's a controls engineer who thinks in feedback loops, failure modes, and root-cause analysis. Plant-floor troubleshooting vs. a conspiracy that stretches from Detroit's riverfront to ag biotech boardrooms.

Sometimes the most dangerous person in the room is the one who actually understands how things work.

The Stakes

When thousands fall ill at America's premier electronic music festival, the CDC sees an outbreak. The FBI sees terrorism. Knox Ramsey sees the digital fingerprints in the data—anomalies that point to something far more sophisticated than contamination.

Racing a ticking clock and federal inertia, Knox traces the attack through layers of automation—from field sensors to enterprise systems. Whoever controls the recipe controls the food supply. And they're just getting started.
